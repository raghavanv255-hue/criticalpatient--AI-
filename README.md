AbstractBackgroundModern healthcare environments require continuous, high-vigilance monitoring of patient vital signs to ensure timely medical intervention. However, healthcare professionals often manage multiple patients simultaneously, creating a cognitive overload that can lead to delayed responses when a seemingly stable patient experiences sudden, life-threatening physiological deterioration.ObjectiveTo address this challenge, this project develops an AI-Powered Critical Patient Monitoring & Response Intelligence System. The system is designed to continuously analyze patient health data, automatically identify abnormalities, dynamically prioritize critical cases, and provide actionable insights to streamline clinical workflows and improve patient safety.Methodology & Core InnovationsThe proposed system integrates Artificial Intelligence (AI), Healthcare Informatics, Real-Time Streaming Data, and Decision Support Analytics. The architecture consists of three core components:Continuous Monitoring Module: Tracks real-time vital signs including heart rate, blood pressure, oxygen saturation ($SpO_2$), and temperature.AI-Based Abnormality & Risk Engine: Utilizes machine learning algorithms to detect subtle, anomalous deviations in vitals and assess overall patient risk levels.Dynamic Prioritization & Alert System: Triages patients based on severity, ensuring that healthcare providers are instantly alerted to the most critical cases first.Results & DeliverablesThe primary deliverable is a functional prototype featuring an interactive monitoring and analytics dashboard. This interface visualizes real-time patient data, highlights high-risk alerts, and provides intelligent response assistance to guide clinical decision-making.Conclusion & Future ScopeBy automating risk assessment and triaging, this system mitigates human oversight, optimizes hospital resource allocation, and significantly reduces response times during medical emergencies. Future iterations will expand the system’s capabilities by integrating with the Internet of Medical Things (IoMT), wearable health devices, smart hospital infrastructure, and predictive analytics for preventative healthcare management.
